§ 01Brand Architecture

An endorsed house of brands.

Each Crosby community has earned its own name and character — and today they don't share a visual system: three residential scripts, two near-identical crests, a heritage fountain mark, and two modern commercial marks. The fix isn't to erase them. It's endorsement: let every place keep its identity, and tie them together with one shared signature — “A Crosby Community” — plus shared navy/blue accents, type, and voice.

§ 05Consistency Roadmap

How to bring the family together.

Sequenced for quick wins first — no property has to lose its identity, and the whole thing can roll out over time.

  1. Draw the endorsement signature. One “A Crosby Community” lockup (parent monogram + line, navy/blue) that clips onto any property mark — the single asset that unifies everything.
  2. Start with the two commercial assets. Sanctuary Office Park adopts the Crosby mark directly; Gulf South keeps its strong mark and adds the endorsement. Fastest, highest-visibility wins.
  3. Systemize the two Metairie crests together. Metairie Lake & Plaza are near-identical heraldry — re-cut both in one clean crest system, distinguished only by name and accent.
  4. Unify the three Northshore residential scripts. The Sanctuary, Mandeville Lake, and Lakeside each keep their signature color, under one shared supporting typeface and layout.
  5. Modernize the two oldest marks last. The Sanctuary and DeLimon carry the most heritage — refresh carefully, preserving the burgundy/gold and est.-1981 cues.

One shared voice, everywhere

Across all eight, the Crosby voice holds: rooted, warm, specific, understated. Residential communities lean warm and lifestyle-led; commercial leans confident and data-led — but all sound like the same family firm that has been here since 1954.
