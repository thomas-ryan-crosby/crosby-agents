§ 01Foundation

A developer measured in generations, not quarters.

Founded in 1954 by John L. Crosby, Sr., Crosby Development Company builds and stewards residential, commercial, and industrial communities across Greater New Orleans and the Northshore. For seventy-two years the firm has done one thing consistently: build places meant to last — then stay to look after them.

We don't build to sell. We build to belong — and we're still here to prove it.

Positioning

The permanence play

For families and businesses who value roots over the transaction, Crosby is the multigenerational Louisiana developer that builds and keeps its communities. Competitors trade buildings; Crosby tends places — the same name on the cornerstone and on the management office, decade after decade.

§ 04The Marks

One mark, built from interlocking frames.

The Crosby signature pairs an interlocking “C” monogram — a navy square and a blue beveled frame that read as construction, drawing, structure — with a bold squared wordmark. It's the primary identity and appears on everything.

Primary lockup · full color on white

On dark grounds, use a white containment field*

Do keep clear space of at least the monogram's height on every side.
Do hold the two-color navy + blue; the monogram may stand alone as a compact mark.
Don't recolor, add gradients/shadows, or stretch the lockup.
Don't place the color logo directly on navy or on a busy photo.

Production gap to close

*A true single-color reversed (white) knockout and a solid one-color navy version should be produced from the vector master (CDC_Logo.eps / .pdf) for dark grounds, embroidery, signage, and single-color print. The files on hand are full-color raster only.

§ 05House of Crosby

A strong parent, a scattered family.

§ 06Color

A disciplined two-color system.

The identity lives in exactly two brand colors, taken straight from the logo — a deep indigo navy for authority and structure, a cornflower blue for openness (sky, and Lake Pontchartrain). Cool neutrals do the rest. Restraint is the point: two colors, held consistently, read as engineered.

§ 07Typography

Squared and technical, like the mark.

The logotype is a bold, squared geometric sans — so headlines echo it: a technical, engineered sans (DIN / Bahnschrift family), set tight and often uppercase. A neutral sans keeps text clear, and a monospace handles the numbers — rents, dimensions, dates — like a surveyor's field book.

Display & Headlines

Aa

DIN · Bahnschrift · squared grotesque

Headlines, stats, labels. Structural and confident — the sound of the monogram. Uppercase for the boldest lines, tight tracking. Recommended brand face: a licensed DIN/Eurostile-style geometric (e.g. Chivo, Saira, or DIN Next) to match the logotype in production.

§ 08Imagery

Real places, real people, honest structure.

Photography should feel lived-in and local. The recurring graphic motif is architectural / survey linework — the same frames-and-contours logic as the monogram (see the cover), always in navy or blue.

§ 10Tagline

Retire the line with an expiration date.

The current line — “…For Over 65 Years” — was already true seven years ago and undercounts the real number (72). A brand built on permanence shouldn't carry a number that quietly goes stale. Lead with the year; it never expires.
