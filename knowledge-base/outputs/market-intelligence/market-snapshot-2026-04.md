## Headlines

- **Gulf South Commerce Park crosses into active operations — Blue Steel commits $22M warehouse.** Love's Travel Stop opened at I-12/LA-1088 on March 12, 2026 — the first operating business at GSCP. More significantly, Blue Steel Development (Florida-based industrial REIT developer) purchased a 12-acre parcel and is building a 200,000 SF multi-tenant warehouse ("Steel 12") for an estimated $22M (~$110/SF construction cost). This is the first third-party developer to commit capital at GSCP, validating the I-12 corridor industrial thesis. With ~250 acres of development-ready sites remaining and ~1,000 acres ultimately planned, GSCP has tangible leasing and development momentum for the first time.

- **Mandeville apartment rents surging +13.6% year-over-year — Northshore residential market tightening.** Average 1BR rents in Mandeville have risen to ~$1,275/mo, with 2BR at $1,461+/mo. Limited rental inventory and continued Northshore population growth are driving above-inflation rent increases. This is a significant tailwind for Mandeville Lake Apartments — once rent roll data is loaded. The contrast with Metairie (rents flat, vacancy rising to 9.1%) reflects the divergent supply dynamics between the two markets.

- **Northshore office asking rates are lower than prior baseline indicated — Sanctuary's premium needs context.** Fresh LoopNet/Crexi research shows Mandeville office asking rates of $17–$22/SF/yr (market avg ~$18.39/SF), and Covington averaging ~$22/SF. The prior baseline estimate of $26.50–$30.00/SF overstated the submarket. At a blended active rate of $25.75/SF, Sanctuary Office Park Building 1 is running 17–29% above current Northshore asking rates. This premium is justified by full-service structure, near-100% occupancy, and campus positioning — but it signals the importance of retention-focused renewal strategy as tenants can theoretically find cheaper options.

- **New 150-unit luxury apartment development announced in Metairie — pipeline risk for Metairie properties.** A new luxury apartment complex has been announced for Metairie, adding to a market that already shows rising vacancy (9.1%, up 10.6% YoY). Crosby's Metairie assets (Metairie Lake: 108 units, Metairie Plaza: 66 units) are currently priced below market — an occupancy buffer — but the combination of rising vacancy and new luxury supply limits near-term pricing power.

## Watch Items

1. **⚠️ Suite 302 re-leasing — asking rate recalibration required.** Security National vacates June 30, 2026. The suite was at $29.50/SF — but Mandeville asking rates run $17–$22/SF for comparable standalone space. A realistic asking rate for the 655 SF suite is likely $22–$25/SF (campus premium over standalone market, but well below prior tenant rate). The prior Vacancy Marketing proposal of $30.37–$32.44/SF is above-market and may impair leasing velocity. **→ Flag: Vacancy Marketing Agent.**

2. **⚠️ URGENT renewals — Bayou CPR and Nirvana Massage (Jul 4, 2026 deadline).** Both tenants require 180-day notice to cancel/terminate; the deadline is 94 days away. If no notice is sent by July 4, both leases auto-renew through December 31, 2026. Bayou CPR ($20.76/SF) is at market; Nirvana ($26.20/SF) is above market but a 13-year tenant. Lease Intelligence Agent should prepare renewal proposals — renewal is likely the right outcome for both, but rates should be validated against current comps. **→ Flag: Lease Intelligence Agent.**

3. **Northshore office asking-rate recalibration.** The prior baseline estimate of $26.50–$30.00/SF for Northshore office does not align with current LoopNet/Crexi asking rates of $17–$22/SF. The difference may reflect full-service vs. NNN lease structure (Sanctuary's gross leases have higher all-in value than NNN asking rates). However, the gap warrants a property-specific comp analysis to confirm Sanctuary's true market position. **→ Recommend: Property-Specific Mode comp pull for Sanctuary Building 1.**

4. **Metairie vacancy rising — Metairie Lake rate review opportunity.** At ~$1,000 entry rate (25.7% below market avg), Metairie Lake is likely over-occupied relative to market. With the Metairie market showing 9.1% vacancy, even moderate rate increases on renewals would keep Metairie Lake highly competitive. **→ Flag: Residential Leasing Agent.**

5. **Mandeville Lake Apartments — data load critical.** With Mandeville rents up 13.6% YoY, this is likely the most under-analyzed asset in the portfolio. Rent roll data is listed as "pending." Without it, the Residential Leasing Agent cannot identify optimization opportunities in the fastest-appreciating market in the portfolio. **→ Priority action: Clerical Data Agent load of Mandeville Lake rent roll.**

6. **Gulf South Commerce Park — Blue Steel lease-up as comp benchmark.** Blue Steel's Steel 12 warehouse (200,000 SF, $22M investment) will create the first real multi-tenant industrial lease comps on the I-12 corridor. Crosby should monitor asking and executed lease rates as this building is marketed. If Blue Steel achieves $8–$10+/SF, it validates pricing for any future improved-space offerings at GSCP. Target for monitoring: Q3 2026.
