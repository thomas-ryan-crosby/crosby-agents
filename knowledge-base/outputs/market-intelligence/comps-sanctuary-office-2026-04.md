## Strategic Recommendation

1. **Narrow the asking range.** The current proposed range ($30.37–$32.44) is wide. Based on comps, recommend **$29.50–$31.00/SF/year** as the listed range. This keeps Crosby above the market median (reflecting the campus premium) without pricing the suite into territory that risks extended vacancy.

2. **Lead with $30.00/SF in marketing copy.** At 655 SF, this translates to **$1,637/month** — a round, clean number that's easy to communicate and represents a slight increase over the prior tenant's rate. The full-service structure (Crosby pays utilities and janitorial) is a significant competitive advantage — most comparable properties use Modified Gross or NNN, meaning the tenant's all-in cost at Sanctuary may actually be comparable or lower.

3. **Highlight the full-service advantage.** Most comps in the $26–$30 range are Modified Gross or NNN. After adding utility and CAM estimates, a tenant at Sanctuary's $30/SF full-service may be paying the same all-in cost as a tenant at $26/SF NNN elsewhere. This should be featured prominently in all marketing materials.

4. **Monitor leasing velocity.** If no qualified inquiries within 45 days of listing, consider reducing to $28.50–$29.50/SF. At 655 SF, each $1/SF reduction is only ~$55/month — a small concession to avoid extended vacancy.

5. **Suite 302 is a small-suite premium opportunity.** Sub-1,000 SF office suites are relatively scarce on the Northshore. Many comparable buildings only offer suites above 1,000 SF (see St. Tammany Corporate Park). The 655 SF size targets solo practitioners, satellite offices, and small professional firms — a segment with steady demand and limited supply.
