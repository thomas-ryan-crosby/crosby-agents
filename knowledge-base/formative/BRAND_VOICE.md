## 3. Voice by Audience

### Residential Tenants (Apartments, Townhomes)
- **Tone:** Warm, community-oriented, welcoming
- **Focus:** Quality of life, convenience, community features, responsive management
- **Avoid:** Corporate jargon, pressure tactics, overly formal language
- **Example opening:** *"We're glad to have you as part of the [Property Name] community..."*

### Commercial Tenants (Sanctuary Office Park)
- **Tone:** Professional, direct, partnership-oriented
- **Focus:** Location advantages, professional community, reliable infrastructure, responsive management
- **Avoid:** Overly casual language, excessive superlatives
- **Example opening:** *"Thank you for your continued tenancy at Sanctuary Office Park..."*

### HOA Members (Lakeside Village, The Sanctuary, DeLimon Place)
- **Tone:** Community steward — collaborative, respectful, informative
- **Focus:** Community standards, shared amenities, property values, quality of life
- **Avoid:** Accusatory language, legalistic tone in routine communications
- **Example opening:** *"As part of the [Community Name] community, we want to keep you informed..."*

### Prospective Tenants / Leasing Prospects
- **Tone:** Inviting, confident, informative
- **Focus:** What makes the property unique, lifestyle benefits, specific features
- **Avoid:** High-pressure language, vague superlatives ("world-class," "best in class")
- **Example headline:** *"Professional Office Space in the Heart of Mandeville's Business District"*

## 4. Writing Standards

### Always
- Use full company name on first reference: *Crosby Development Company, L.L.C.*
- Short reference thereafter: *Crosby Development*
- Use specific, accurate figures (SF, rent amounts, dates) — never approximate
- Include Fair Housing language on all residential materials (see below)
- Spell out Louisiana (not "LA" in body copy — reserved for mailing addresses)

### Never
- Use hyperbole: "luxury," "world-class," "premier," "unparalleled" without specific support
- Make claims that can't be verified: "best location," "most amenities"
- Refer to race, national origin, religion, familial status, disability, or any protected class
- Use all-caps for emphasis (use bold instead)
- Use exclamation points in formal communications

### Numbers & Formatting
- Rent: $1,331/mo or $1,331 per month (not $1331/mo or $1,331.00/mo in casual copy)
- Square footage: 610 SF or 610 square feet (not 610 sq ft)
- Dates: June 30, 2026 (not 6/30/26 in formal letters)
- Percentages: 3% per annum (not 3% p.a. or 3%/yr)

## 5. Property-Specific Voice Notes

### Sanctuary Office Park
Position as the professional services hub of the Northshore. Emphasize:
- Central Mandeville location, easy I-12 access
- Established professional community (medical, legal, financial services tenants)
- Management responsiveness
- Competitive rates for the area

### Mandeville Lake Apartments / Metairie Plaza / Metairie Lake
Position around community lifestyle and convenience. Emphasize:
- Lake/park access and natural surroundings (where applicable)
- Proximity to schools, shopping, dining
- Well-maintained grounds and amenities
- Responsive maintenance and management

### Lakeside Village Townhomes
Position as a premium HOA community with active management. Emphasize:
- Townhome and cottage variety, range of sizes
- HOA-maintained amenities and common areas
- Mandeville schools and community
- Strong long-term property values

### Gulf South Commerce Park
Position for industrial and logistics users. Emphasize:
- Interstate access and regional logistics advantages
- Acreage flexibility and custom site development
- Industrial zoning and infrastructure readiness
- Louisiana business incentives (enterprise zone, tax credits — verify with current data)

## 6. Fair Housing Compliance

> *Crosby Development Company, L.L.C. is an equal opportunity housing provider. We do not discriminate on the basis of race, color, national origin, religion, sex, familial status, disability, or any other protected class under applicable federal, state, or local law.*

> *Equal Opportunity Housing Provider.*

**Louisiana-specific note:** Louisiana Fair Housing Law (R.S. 51:2601 et seq.) mirrors federal protections. Age and sexual orientation protections may apply in certain jurisdictions — confirm with legal counsel for specific properties.
