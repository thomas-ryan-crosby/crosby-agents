# Fair Housing Compliance Guide
## Crosby Development — Claude Agent System

**Applies to:** All agents producing residential marketing materials
**Last Reviewed:** April 2026
**Authority:** Fair Housing Act (42 U.S.C. § 3604), Louisiana Fair Housing Law

> ⚠️ This guide is for marketing compliance reference only. It does not constitute legal advice. Consult qualified legal counsel for specific Fair Housing questions or disputes.

## Required Inclusions in All Residential Marketing Materials

### 1. Equal Housing Opportunity Statement
Include at the footer of every brochure, flyer, listing description, email, and advertisement:

> *"Crosby Development Company, L.L.C. is an Equal Housing Opportunity provider."*

Abbreviated version (for space-constrained formats):
> *"Equal Housing Opportunity"*

### 2. Equal Housing Opportunity Logo
All print materials must include the EHO logo (house with equal sign).
- Logo file location: `/knowledge-base/assets/logos/eho-logo.png`
- Minimum size: 0.5" x 0.5" in print materials
- Placeholder in agent outputs: `[EHO LOGO]`

### 3. Fair Housing Tagline (Optional but Recommended)
For display ads and signage:
> *"We do business in accordance with the Federal Fair Housing Law."*

## Protected Classes — Never Discriminate On

Federal law protects against discrimination based on:
- **Race**
- **Color**
- **National origin**
- **Religion**
- **Sex** (includes gender identity and sexual orientation under HUD guidance)
- **Familial status** (families with children under 18, pregnant persons)
- **Disability** (physical or mental)

Louisiana state law adds:
- **Source of income** (in some parishes — verify current applicability)

## Prohibited Language — Never Use in Marketing Materials

### Demographic Targeting Language
❌ "Perfect for young couples"
❌ "Ideal for families"
❌ "Great for seniors"
❌ "Professional community"
❌ "Quiet adult community"
❌ "Adult living"
❌ "No children"
❌ "Christian community"

### Neighborhood / Demographic Descriptors
❌ Any description of neighborhood demographics (racial, ethnic, national origin)
❌ "Safe neighborhood" as a coded demographic reference (use specific safety features instead, e.g., "gated access," "security cameras")

### Disability-Related Language
❌ "No wheelchairs"
❌ "Normal" or "healthy" residents
❌ "No mental illness"
❌ Use "accessible features available" for ADA-relevant properties

### Proximity Language That Can Imply Discrimination
❌ "Walking distance to..." — can exclude persons with mobility disabilities
✅ Use instead: "Minutes from..." or "Conveniently located near..."

---

## Permitted Language — Safe Alternatives

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|---|---|
| "Perfect for young professionals" | "Convenient to [employment center]" |
| "Ideal for families" | "Near [school name] and family-friendly parks" |
| "Great for empty nesters" | "Low-maintenance living in a quiet community" |
| "Safe neighborhood" | "Gated community with security cameras" |
| "Walking distance" | "Minutes from" / "short drive to" |

## Social Media & Digital Advertising

### Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram)
Residential rental ads must be created under Meta's **Special Ad Category: Housing**.
- This restricts demographic targeting by age, gender, zip code, and neighborhood
- Do not use audience segments based on demographics or protected characteristics
- The social-media-agent will tag all residential ads as Housing category

### Google Ads
- Standard policy: do not target or exclude based on protected class characteristics
- Broad geographic targeting is acceptable; avoid hyper-local targeting that could approximate demographic targeting

## Louisiana-Specific Requirements

- All leases must include Louisiana Residential Landlord-Tenant Act notices
- Louisiana does not require source of income protection statewide, but verify at time of lease execution
- St. Tammany Parish and Jefferson Parish: confirm any parish-level Fair Housing ordinances annually
