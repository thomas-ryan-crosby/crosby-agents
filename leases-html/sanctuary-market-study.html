A competitive-intelligence brief for the Sanctuary leasing team: where the Northshore office market stands, who you compete against, what tenants actually pay, and how to position Sanctuary's full-service model to win deals.

Bottom line for Sanctuary

Sanctuary is, in practical terms, the dominant Class A office campus in Mandeville — its ~96,000 SF rivals the entire Class A inventory the rest of the submarket can list. While the parish runs a 13.9% office vacancy, Sanctuary sits near 99% leased. The headline gap between Sanctuary's ~$27 full-service rate and the market's ~$18–$22 "asking" rate is largely an illusion of quoting convention: those are NNN base rents that exclude $4–$12/SF of pass-throughs. On an apples-to-apples total-occupancy-cost basis, Sanctuary is competitive — and far more predictable.

2 · Where Sanctuary sits — the rate reality

The market quotes office at $18–$22/SF, and Sanctuary quotes ~$27. That is not a $5–$9 premium. The quoted Northshore numbers are overwhelmingly triple-net (NNN) base rents, to which the tenant separately pays its pro-rata share of property taxes, building insurance, and CAM (common-area maintenance — which includes janitorial, shared utilities, security, landscaping, parking-lot upkeep). Those pass-throughs typically add $4–$12/SF/yr, and they arrive as an annual reconciliation the tenant cannot fully predict.

3 · The full-service advantage (why it closes deals)

Gross/full-service leases shift operating-cost risk from tenant to landlord and are widely regarded as the more tenant-friendly structure because they let a tenant budget with certainty. CAM is consistently the most contentious, dispute-prone line in NNN office leases because the category is broad and the landlord controls the spend. Sanctuary removes that friction entirely.

  • Predictability — one fixed, all-in number with a known 3% annual step. No surprise true-ups; CFOs and office managers love this.
  • Speed to signature — no negotiation over expense definitions, gross-up clauses, audit rights, or caps. The lease is simpler and faster to execute.
  • Net cost often lower — a $20 NNN quote frequently lands at $26–$32 all-in; Sanctuary's $27 includes everything, including in-suite janitorial most landlords push back on the tenant.
  • Quality signal — full-service, professionally managed, with janitorial and HVAC handled, reads as Class A service, reinforcing the rate.

6 · Demand drivers & tenant mix

  • Population & in-migration. St. Tammany is among Louisiana's fastest-growing parishes — ~272,000 residents, up ~19.6% since 2010 and ~13% over the last decade (~1.4%/yr) — fed by steady out-migration from New Orleans/Jefferson seeking lower weather/hurricane risk. Affluent, well-insured households underpin demand.
  • Corporate precedent. Chevron relocated its regional headquarters from downtown New Orleans to a Covington office park in 2008 — the anchor proof that national tenants will site the Northshore.
  • Healthcare is the engine. St. Tammany Health System (partnered with Ochsner) and LCMC's Lakeview Regional are in active expansion (e.g., new Tulane Surgery Clinic Northshore), driving sustained medical-office demand. Sanctuary's Floor-3 medical/wellness concentration is aligned with the strongest demand vertical in the parish.
  • Professional services. Insurance, finance, title, legal and back-office users dominate the small-to-mid suite demand Sanctuary serves best (200–2,500 SF).

7 · Supply pipeline

New office supply is concentrated in Covington's master-planned nodes, not Mandeville. River Chase II (50,000 SF, 2024) absorbs Stirling's own HQ. Parish office parks cited as primed for further development include Tammaned, River Chase, Nor du Lac, Bilten Park and Northpark. Implication for Sanctuary: limited new Class A competition in the immediate Mandeville/Causeway submarket — reinforcing Sanctuary's scarcity position close to the bridge.

8 · Vacancy duration & leasing velocity

Granular days-on-market for Northshore office isn't publicly tracked, but the structural picture is clear: a 13.9% parish vacancy with a thin pool of available Class A space (Mandeville lists only ~50,000 SF available across ~19 spaces, averaging ~2,650 SF). Quality, full-service small-to-mid suites in a Causeway-proximate Class A campus are the scarcest product type — which is precisely why Sanctuary holds ~99% occupancy while the broader market carries vacancy. Practically: well-priced Sanctuary suites should out-lease the market on velocity; the risk is not demand, it's having product to show.

9 · Leasing playbook for Sanctuary

  • Lead with total cost, not base rent. Always reframe a competitor's "$18–$22" into all-in dollars with taxes/insurance/CAM/janitorial added back. Carry the comparison table above.
  • Target CAM-fatigued tenants. Prospect medical, insurance, title and professional firms currently in NNN space who've just absorbed a year-end reconciliation. The "one predictable number" message lands hardest there.
  • Own the Class A scarcity narrative in Mandeville. You are the Class A inventory on this side of the lake near the Causeway. Price with confidence; hold the 3% escalation.
  • Court the healthcare vertical. Align Floor-3 medical/wellness availability with the STHS/Ochsner/Lakeview growth story.
  • Co-broke deliberately. Equip Stirling & NAI Latter & Blum agents with a Sanctuary availability + cost-comparison one-pager.
  • Defend renewals on certainty. Existing tenants stay because they never get a surprise bill — make that explicit at renewal.
