Bottom line for Sanctuary

The Northshore office market is deep but slow to absorb: 116 office availabilities compete today, and the median one has already sat ~142 days (4.7 months) — with a long tail where the top quartile sits 14+ months and roughly 1 in 10 sits 2+ years. Sanctuary, at ~99% leased, is outperforming that market by a wide margin. On price, Sanctuary's full-service ~$24–$27 is in line with the tiny full-service Class A comp set ($24–$28) — and full-service is rare here: only 3 of 60 structurally-detailed listings offer it. The apparent "discount" at NNN buildings ($16–$19 base) evaporates once taxes, insurance, CAM and janitorial are added back.

2 · How long are vacancies lasting? (the real number)

This is the question public sources can't answer — but the LACDB record can. Across the 116 currently-active office listings, the median time on market is ~142 days (4.7 months), but the distribution has a heavy right tail: the top quartile has sat 14+ months, and roughly 1 in 10 has sat 2+ years. With 766 leased comps behind them, these availabilities sit in a market that absorbs office slowly and selectively — many blocks carry for a year or more before they transact.

What this means for Sanctuary

Holding ~99% occupancy in a market where the median vacancy lingers 7+ months — and where closed deals took ~10 months to find — is the single most powerful proof point in your pitch and your renewals. Tenants are not easy to replace in this market; Sanctuary's retention and velocity are the exception, not the rule. Price and hold with confidence; the downside risk of a vacancy here is measured in quarters, not weeks, which is exactly why the full-service, low-friction model that keeps tenants in place is worth a premium.

6 · Demand drivers

  • Healthcare is the engine. Medical office recurs throughout the listings (Pinnacle, Mandeville Medical, 800 N Causeway, Hollycrest). St. Tammany Health System (with Ochsner) and LCMC's Lakeview Regional are expanding — Sanctuary's Floor-3 medical/wellness concentration is aligned with the parish's strongest demand vertical.
  • New Orleans → Northshore migration. St. Tammany (~272k, +13% in a decade) keeps absorbing households and businesses crossing the lake; Chevron's 2008 HQ move to a Covington office park set the corporate precedent.
  • Professional & small-suite demand. The median LACDB office listing is small (≈2,500–3,000 SF) — insurance, title, finance, legal and executive-suite users. That is precisely the 200–2,500 SF band Sanctuary fills best.

7 · Supply pipeline

New Class A supply is concentrated in Covington's master-planned nodes (River Chase, Northpark, the Hwy-21 corridor, New Camellia/Tammany West), not in Mandeville. River Chase II (~50k SF, Stirling's own new HQ) and several "Under Construction" Covington listings appear in the data. Implication: limited new Class A competition in the immediate Mandeville/Causeway submarket — reinforcing Sanctuary's scarcity position near the bridge.

8 · Leasing playbook for Sanctuary

  • Lead with all-in cost, not base rent. Reframe any "$16–$19" NNN quote into total dollars with taxes/insurance/CAM/janitorial added back. Carry the comparison table.
  • Sell the velocity gap. "The median office listing out there sits ~5 months — and the slow quartile lingers past a year — while we run 99% full. That stability is what you're buying." Devastating on renewals.
  • Own full-service scarcity. Only three buildings in the parish offer it; you're priced with them. It's a feature, not a premium.
  • Own Class A in Mandeville. The cheap Mandeville quotes are Class B. You are the Class A, full-service, Causeway-adjacent option — price accordingly and hold the 3% step.
  • Target CAM-fatigued & healthcare tenants. Medical/insurance/title firms in NNN space post-reconciliation are your warmest prospects.
  • Co-broke with Stirling & KPG. Equip them with the Sanctuary cost-comparison one-pager.
